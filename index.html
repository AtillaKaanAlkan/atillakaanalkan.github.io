Hi! I'm a researcher in natural language processing and machine learning at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics. How much of what we know about the Universe lies scattered across millions of papers, and could machines help us read, connect, and make sense of it all? That question drives my main research, which uses computational methods to extract information from the astrophysics literature and turn it into structured, living knowledge. It grew out of my doctoral thesis, where I worked on automatically capturing what papers report about celestial transient phenomena such as gamma-ray bursts, gravitational waves, black holes, and active galactic nuclei.

I'm also fascinated by what large language models might bring to science itself. Can they help researchers generate genuinely new hypotheses? Do they learn about language the way we humans do, and how are concepts, astronomical ones included, represented and transformed across the layers of a network? Can we ever truly explain why a model makes a given prediction? Beyond research, I love communicating about science. Together with governor Jean-Martin Jaspers, I wrote a popular science book on natural language processing, machine learning, and their applications for the French public administration.
