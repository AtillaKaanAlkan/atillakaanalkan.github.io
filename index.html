Hi! I'm a researcher in Natural Language Processing and Artificial Intelligence at the Center for Astrophysics | Harvard & Smithsonian (CfA). How much of what we know about the Universe lies scattered across millions of papers — and could machines help us read, connect, and make sense of it all? That question drives my main research: using NLP to extract information from the astrophysics literature and turn it into structured, living knowledge. It grew out of my doctoral thesis, where I worked on automatically capturing what papers report about celestial transient phenomena — gamma-ray bursts, gravitational waves, black holes, and active galactic nuclei.

I'm also fascinated by what large language models might bring to science itself. Can they help researchers generate genuinely new hypotheses? Do they learn about language the way we humans do — and how are concepts, astronomical ones included, represented and transformed across the layers of a network? Can we ever truly explain why a model makes a given prediction? Beyond research, I love communicating about science: I co-wrote a popular-science book with governor Jean-Martin Jaspers on NLP, AI, and their applications for the French public administration.
