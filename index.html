Hi! I'm a researcher in Natural Language Processing and Artificial Intelligence at the Center for Astrophysics | Harvard & Smithsonian (CfA). My main research uses NLP to extract information from the astrophysics literature and turn it into structured knowledge — enriching how the field organizes and connects what it already knows. This grew out of my doctoral thesis, where I worked on extracting information about celestial transient phenomena such as gamma-ray bursts, gravitational waves, black holes, and active galactic nuclei.

As part of UniverseTBD, I also explore how large language models can support scientific discovery. A central question drives this work: can language models help scientists generate new hypotheses — proposing ideas that are genuinely novel, plausible, and worth testing? I'm equally fascinated by what these models learn about language itself: do their internal representations of meaning resemble our own as humans? How are concepts — astronomical ones, for instance — encoded and transformed across the layers of a network? And can we explain why a model makes a given prediction?

Outside of research, I love communicating about science. I co-wrote a popular-science book with governor Jean-Martin Jaspers on NLP, AI, and their applications for the French public administration — with the goal of making these technologies accessible to everyone.
